### SurveyPlanet (Evaluated February 2026)
**Date:** 2026-02-13  
**Outcome:** Evaluation changed from PASS â†’ PURSUE after deep analysis  
**Key Learnings:**

1. **Never-raised prices in 15 years = massive opportunity**
   - Pattern: Organic/SEO businesses undermonetize systematically
   - Competitive research revealed 45-95% underpricing
   - Validated +$252k ARR opportunity from single pricing change

2. **P&L line items can hide $200k+ in economics**
   - $234k "Outside Services" was CEO compensation
   - Changed SDE from 37.7% (FAIL) â†’ 54.2% (PASS)
   - Learning: Always interrogate vague expenses >$50k before scoring

3. **Declining growth requires context**
   - -8% YoY growth initially seemed fatal
   - Deep dive revealed: Never raised prices, never emailed users, cut marketing 95%
   - These are FIXABLE underexecution issues, not structural decline
   - Learning: Ask "WHY declining?" not just "IS IT declining?"

4. **Questions document contains gold**
   - Founder Q&A revealed MRR trends, churn data, untapped opportunities
   - Changed entire evaluation trajectory
   - Learning: Always read questions doc FIRST if exists

5. **"Never tried X" needs nuance**
   - "Never emailed 1M user database" = REAL opportunity (owned channel)
   - "Never tried outbound sales" = PIE-IN-THE-SKY (unowned channel)
   - Learning: Distinguish owned vs. unowned distribution

6. **Customer complaints <5% = pricing power**
   - Founder: "We rarely get complaints about pricing"
   - Organic traffic (78%) = price-insensitive customers
   - Learning: Low complaint rate + organic acquisition = can raise prices aggressively

7. **AI displacement timeline matters**
   - Survey tools ARE vulnerable to LLMs
   - But 15-year brand + 1M users + organic traffic = defensible for 2-3 years
   - Learning: Don't just flag AI risk, assess timeline AND moat

### Underexecution vs. Structural Decline
**Underexecution (Fixable):**
- Never raised prices while competitors did
- Never activated obvious channels (email, partnerships)
- Declining marketing spend without replacement
- Founder burnout / reduced time investment

**Structural (Fatal):**
- Competitor disruption (cheaper/better product launched)
- Market shrinking (category search volume down)
- Technology obsolescence (Flash in 2020)
- Regulatory changes

**Test:** Google Trends for category keywords, competitor research

### Strong Distribution Moats
- Existing partnerships generating 30%+ of leads
- Viral product mechanics (survey completions â†’ sign-ups)
- Embedded in workflows (ATS integration)
- Geographic advantage (only Spanish/Portuguese option)

**Valuation Impact:** Justifies premium pricing multiple

## Sector-Specific Insights

### Pre-Employment Assessment Tools
- **Competition:** Very crowded market (TestGorilla, Codility, HackerRank, etc.)
- **Differentiation:** Geography (LatAm) or niche (technical + soft skills)
- **Pricing:** Wide range ($20-200/mo per seat)
- **AI Risk:** HIGH for automated grading, MEDIUM for test creation
- **Key Success Factor:** ATS integrations drive 30%+ of revenue

### Survey/Form Tools
- **Competition:** Dominated by SurveyMonkey, Typeform, Google Forms
- **Differentiation:** Unlimited features (vs. paywalled) or specialization
- **Pricing:** $20-70/mo for pro plans
- **AI Risk:** HIGH for survey creation/analysis (2-3 year horizon)
- **Key Success Factor:** Distribution moat (viral loops, embedded in workflows)

## Framework Improvements Needed

### 1. AI Displacement Risk Framework
**Current:** Binary flag (yes/no)  
**Needed:** Multi-factor scoring
- Displacement timeline (6mo, 1yr, 2yr, 5yr+)
- Defensibility factors (brand, distribution, data moat)
- Category-specific vulnerabilities

### 2. Churn Benchmarks by Category
**Current:** No specific thresholds  
**Needed:** Category benchmarks
- B2B SaaS: 5-7% monthly acceptable
- Prosumer tools: 10-15% monthly acceptable
- Consumer: 20%+ monthly common
- Need to flag abnormal churn for category

### 3. Team Transition Heuristics
**Current:** Vague guidance on founder replaceability  
**Needed:** Structured assessment
- Founder technical depth (10+ years = hard to replace)
- Knowledge documentation state
- Team bench strength
- Customer relationship dependency

### 4. Price Increase Shock Modeling
**Current:** Assumes flat 5% churn  
**Needed:** Cohort-based modeling
- Long-term customers (2+ years): 0-2% churn
- Medium-term (6-24 months): 5-10% churn
- New customers (<6 months): 15-25% churn
- Weighted average for realistic impact

### 5. Competitive Moat Assessment
**Current:** Lightweight narrative  
**Needed:** Structured framework
- Network effects (0-10)
- Switching costs (0-10)
- Brand strength (0-10)
- Proprietary data/tech (0-10)
- Distribution (0-10)
- Composite moat score
